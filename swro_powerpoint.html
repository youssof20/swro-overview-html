What is SWRO?

Seawater Reverse Osmosis Overview

Seawater Reverse Osmosis (SWRO) is an advanced desalination technology that removes salt and other impurities from seawater to produce fresh, potable water.

Key Principle

SWRO uses high pressure to force seawater through semi-permeable membranes, allowing only water molecules to pass through while blocking salt and other contaminants.

Why SWRO?

  • Growing global water scarcity
  • Reliable source independent of rainfall
  • High-quality water production
  • Scalable technology for various applications
  • Continuous operation capability

SWRO Process Flow

Main Process Steps

1. Intake

Seawater collection from ocean or sea

2. Pre-treatment

Remove large particles and prepare water

3. High Pressure

Pressurize water to 800-1000 psi

4. RO Membranes

Separate salt from water

5. Post-treatment

Adjust pH and add minerals

6. Distribution

Deliver clean water to users

Seawater Intake System

First Step: Water Collection

Open Ocean Intake

Direct intake from sea through intake pipes extending into deep water

Advantages: High quality source water, minimal environmental impact

Beach Wells

Horizontal or vertical wells in coastal areas using natural sand filtration

Advantages: Natural pre-filtration, reduced biofouling

Key Components:

  • Intake Screens: Remove large debris and marine life
  • Intake Pumps: Transport seawater to treatment facility
  • Flow Control Systems: Regulate water flow rates
  • Emergency Shutoff: Protection systems for intake

Pre-treatment Process

Preparing Seawater for RO

Pre-treatment Steps:

Coarse Screening

Remove large debris, seaweed, and marine life (>6mm)

Fine Screening

Remove smaller particles and suspended solids (1-3mm)

Chemical Dosing

Add coagulants, flocculants, and disinfectants

Sedimentation

Allow particles to settle in clarifiers

Multimedia Filtration

Remove suspended particles through sand and anthracite filters

Cartridge Filtration

Final polishing to protect RO membranes (5-10 microns)

High Pressure System

The Heart of SWRO

Operating Pressure: 800-1000 psi (55-70 bar)

This high pressure is necessary to overcome the osmotic pressure of seawater (~27 bar) and force water molecules through the RO membranes.

Key Components:

High Pressure Pumps

Centrifugal or positive displacement pumps that pressurize the feed water

Types: Centrifugal multistage, axially split case

Energy Recovery Devices

Recover energy from high-pressure brine stream

Types: Pelton wheels, pressure exchangers, turbochargers

Pressure Regulation

Control valves and pressure relief systems

Function: Maintain optimal operating pressure

Reverse Osmosis Membranes

The Core Separation Technology

Membrane Characteristics:

Parameter Typical Value Purpose
Pore Size 0.0001 microns Block salt ions while allowing water
Salt Rejection 99.4-99.8% Ensure high-quality product water
Operating Pressure 800-1000 psi Drive water through membrane
Recovery Rate 35-50% Percentage of feed water converted

Membrane Configuration:

  • Spiral Wound: Most common, high surface area
  • Pressure Vessels: House 6-8 membrane elements
  • Array Configuration: Multiple stages for optimal performance

Post-treatment Process

Finalizing Water Quality

Why Post-treatment?

RO permeate is very pure but requires adjustment for:

  • pH correction (RO water is slightly acidic)
  • Mineral addition for taste and health
  • Corrosion control in distribution systems
  • Disinfection for storage and distribution

pH Adjustment

Add lime (Ca(OH)₂) or caustic soda (NaOH) to raise pH to 7.5-8.5

Remineralization

Add calcium chloride, magnesium sulfate for taste and nutrition

Corrosion Control

Add phosphates or other inhibitors to protect pipes

Disinfection

Chlorination or UV treatment for microbial control

Energy Recovery Systems

Improving Process Efficiency

Energy Savings: 35-60%

Energy recovery devices can reduce total energy consumption from 6-8 kWh/m³ to 3-4 kWh/m³

Pressure Exchangers

Transfer pressure from brine to feed water using rotating chambers

Efficiency: 95-98%

Pelton Wheels

Turbine-driven system that recovers energy from high-pressure brine

Efficiency: 85-90%

Turbochargers

Centrifugal pump/turbine combination for energy recovery

Efficiency: 80-85%

Benefits:

  • Reduced operating costs
  • Lower carbon footprint
  • Improved plant economics
  • Enhanced sustainability
